 SaaS Product Metrics Framework

This repository outlines a simple framework for measuring the health and growth of a SaaS product.

Understanding the right metrics helps product teams make better decisions about features, growth, and retention.

---

 Core SaaS Metrics

 1. Monthly Active Users (MAU)

Measures how many users actively use the product each month.

Why it matters:
- Shows real product engagement
- Indicates growth or decline

---

 2. Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)

How much it costs to acquire one paying customer.

Formula:

CAC = Total Marketing + Sales Cost / New Customers

Why it matters:
- Shows efficiency of growth
- Helps evaluate marketing channels

---

 3. Customer Lifetime Value (LTV)

Revenue expected from a customer during their relationship with the product.

Why it matters:
- Helps determine sustainable acquisition cost
- Indicates long-term product value

---

 4. Churn Rate

Percentage of customers who stop using the product.

Why it matters:
- High churn signals product problems
- Directly affects growth

---

 5. Conversion Rate

Percentage of users that move from free trial to paid.

Why it matters:
- Indicates product value perception
- Shows onboarding effectiveness

---

 Example Product Dashboard

A healthy SaaS product usually tracks:

- MAU growth
- Trial → Paid conversion
- Churn rate
- Average revenue per user (ARPU)

These metrics together show the true health of a product.

---

 Product Thinking

Good product decisions should always be guided by measurable outcomes.  
Tracking the right metrics helps product teams prioritize improvements that actually impact users and business growth.
